How to be a good Street Photographer.


after buying the right gear you are now half way of becoming an awesome street photographer. The next step will be going out on street and look for your first subject. Here are the compositions that will help you become a master of this craft:

Keep in my that photography is an art. Learn to appreciate the work of others and aim to improve yourself

Lastly, Enjoy and have fun! Take as many photo as you like!


  • The rule of Thirds
  • Sub framing
  • Leading lines
  • Color Theory
  • Long exposure

Compositions:

The Rule of Thirds

One of the common used composition in Photography. It uses the 3x3 frame in your camera and placing the subject on the third line. It is not always a good idea to place your subject on the middle

Sub Framing

One of the fun ways to capture people is to frame them with different object. Think of a window, people walking, cars passing by and etc. The only limitation is your imagination.

Leading lines

Leading lines are lines that appear in a photograph that have been framed and positioned by the photographer to draw the viewer's eye towards a specific point of interest. These lines often draw the viewer’s eye in a specific direction or towards a designated portion of the photograph.

Color Theory

Color theory is the collection of rules and guidelines which designers use to communicate with users through appealing color schemes in visual interfaces. To pick the best colors every time, designers use a color wheel and refer to extensive collected knowledge about human optical ability, psychology, culture and more.

Long Exposure

Long exposure photography is when we are using a much longer shutter speed, and it’s usually used as a specific technique to achieve a certain effect. There’s no defined transition point at which a shutter speed becomes slow enough to define your shooting as long exposure photography.

Conclusion

The most important thing is that you are having fun! Don't stress out about which camera or which composition to use. Just go out there and start taking photos! The more time you spent taking photos the faster you will master it.
